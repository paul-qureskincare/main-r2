What is LED light therapy?

LED means light-emitting diode. LED light therapy is a noninvasive approach that works by emitting infrared lights in different wavelengths/spectrums, which have distinct skincare benefits. Red light is the most frequently used light to promote circulation. Blue light kills bacteria.

During an LED light therapy at-home treatment, the device sends light waves deep into the skin to trigger natural intracellular reactions. Depending on which light is being used, the skin will respond differently. Using the red light, your skin responds by building, strengthening and maximizing cellular structure. Red light is additionally believed to target oil glands and reduce cytokines, which are responsible for inflammation and play a role in chronic acne. If you opt to use the blue light, specific wavelengths stimulate production of oxygen radicals that kill P.acnes bacteria.

And, all of this is done without damaging the skin.

How LED light therapy works

At home LED light therapy devices have been created so you can treat skin problems at home on your own. The light doesn’t cause any burns or skin damage, it is painless with no down time.

LED light therapy harnesses the healing power of light for complete skin rejuvenation. LED light therapy works on a cellular level to tackle a huge range of skin concerns. It is really quite incredible!

What are the benefits of LED light therapy?

People are turning to LED light therapy to fix skin problems such as acne. In fact, it works well for a variety of skin conditions, from eczema, to rosacea, and so much more.

Light therapy has an extensive list of amazing benefits such as;

  • Stimulates collagen production
  • Smooths fine lines and wrinkles
  • Reduces redness and inflammation
  • Prevents breakouts by killing the acne-causing bacteria
  • Minimizes pores by regulating oil production
  • Improves appearance of scarring by promoting skin cell healing
  • Brightens skin and dark spots
  • Smooths skin texture

How to Prepare for LED Light Therapy

It’s always recommended that the skin must be cleansed and clean without any make up before LED light therapy treatment. For in-clinic treatments you will be required to wear protective eyewear to protect your eyes from the light.

LED Light Therapy vs. Microneedling

Micro-needling is another popular facial treatment to rejuvenate the skin. It can improve signs of ageing, acne, scarring and pigmentation.

The needles create microscopic wounds on the top layer of the skin and ignite the body’s natural defence is to repair the wounds, causing collagen regeneration to create new tissue and the result is a fresher, younger looking appearance.

The benefits are the same as LED light therapy, but a little more invasive.

Often facial treatment in-clinics include the combination of LED light and microneedling in one visit to effectively treat multiple skin concerns. The treated skin is exposed to LED light therapy after micro-needling or microdermabrasion to maximise results. LED light is clinically proven to stimulate cellular activity, therefore encouraging collagen production. LED light therapy will further reduce fine lines and wrinkles , improve scarring, clear blemishes, improve skin tone, and repair sun damage. Some experts might label this combination as the “perfect pair”.

At-Home vs. In Clinic

Research and clinical studies have proven that LED light therapy treatments can effectively treat a multitude of skin concerns and provide transformative results. But the results are cumulative, meaning you won’t see long-term benefits from that single salon trip you treat yourself to every so often.

In order to see long-term results then regular appointments are required, and these regular in-clinic trips are not only time consuming, but can be very expensive. If regular appointments aren’t an option, investing in a good quality at-home LED light therapy device could be the answer.

Though in-clinic LED panels are more powerful than at-home devices, it doesn't mean an at-home device won't be effective or offer the same benefits.

At-home LED devices are an excellent way to bring expensive in-clinic treatment to the comfort of your home for a fraction of the price. Remember, LED treatments deliver cumulative results, commitment is key. So this is why particularly at-home LED devices are worth your investment. They allow you to perform regular LED light therapy treatments on your skin at home, which means you’ll be able to see short term results and get long term benefits.
